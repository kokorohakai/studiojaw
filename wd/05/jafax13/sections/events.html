Anime Music Video Contest Rules

Contest Format
Showings
The competing AMVs will be shown once each day of the event. The audience at the Saturday showing will be given ballots to vote for the Audience Choice award.

Categories
AMVs will be organized into categories by the Contest Coordinator, with at least two AMVs competing in each category. Creators may indicate a preferred category on the Entry Form, but the Contest Coordinator has final say on how AMVs will be categorized.
Creators will be notified of how their AMVs have been categorized on June 1.

Judging
AMVs will be judged by a panel of four judges, consisting of at least two AMV creators (who are not participating in the contest). AMVs will be judged based on their technical and artistic merits using a points system. Judging will take place in a private screening before JAFAX.

Awards and Prizes
The following awards will be given by the judges:
-Best of Show
-Best of Category (one for each category)
-Best of Staff

An Audience Choice award will be given by the audience of the Saturday showing.

Honorable Mentions may be given at the judges' discretion.

AMVs created by JAFAX staff and volunteers will compete in the Staff category and are not eligible to win the Best of Show award.

There is no restriction on the number of awards a AMV may win, except for videos competing in the Staff category, which are only eligible to win Best of Staff and Audience Choice.

Audience Choice ballots will be tabulated and all winners will be announced at the end of the Saturday showing.

Prizes will be given to all award winners (except Honorable Mentions), and will be distributed at Closing Ceremonies. The nature and value of prizes has not yet been determined.

Attendance Requirement
To participate in the contest, the creator or a designated proxy must be present at the Saturday showing or Closing Ceremonies.

Content Guidelines
AMVs should meet the following guidelines:

- The AMV must be at least 30 seconds and no more than 6 minutes long.
- At least 66% of the video must be Japanese animation and/or Japanese video game footage.
- At least 66% of the audio must be music.
- No logos, watermarks, or other similar artifacts may be present on the source footage.
- No subtitles may be present on the source footage. Subtitles added by the creator are permitted.
- Credits, bumpers, title screens, watermarks, and other such forms of identification are not allowed. Uniform title cards will be created using the information provided on the Entry Form.
- The AMV must be of sufficient audio and video quality for a theater-type presentation.
- The AMV must not exceed PG-13 content standards as defined by the MPAA.

Exceptions to these rules will be made on a case by case basis at the discretion of the Contest Coordinator. If you aren't sure if your video will be accepted, email the Contest Coordinator at amv@jafax.org.

Submission Rules

General
- AMVs with multiple creators may be submitted, but JAFAX does not guarantee that more than one prize will be awarded per video.

- Creators may submit up to three AMVs. If multiple AMVs from the same creator are placed into the same category, the creator will choose one video to compete, and the rest will be removed from the contest. Participation in a multiple-creator AMV counts toward both of these limits.

- AMVs must have been completed on or after January 1, 2006, and may not have competed in any previous JAFAX AMV Contest.

- AMVs that have won more than three major awards from any AMV contest are not eligible to participate. Major awards include but are not limited to Best of Show, Best of Category, Best Technical, and Audience Choice. Any number of other awards, such as Honorable Mention, Runner Up, etc. are permitted.

- AMVs (with Entry Forms) must be received by May 31st, 2008, 11:59pm.

- AMVs must be original compositions by the individual(s) submitting the AMVs. Submitting another creator's AMV as one's own work or incorporating part or whole of another creator's AMV into a separate AMV without express written consent from the original creator will result in immediate, permanent, and perpetual disqualification from the current and all future JAFAX AMV Contests. However, parody or satire of another creator's video may be permitted on a case-by-case basis.

Technical Specifications
Submitted files must meet the following specifications:

- Video Format: MPEG-2, MPEG-4, or DivX/XviD
- Framerate: 30 frames per second maximum, progressive only (no interlaced video)
- Resolution: 480 pixels maximum vertical, no maximum horizontal
- Pixel Aspect Ratio: 1:1 (square)
- Audio Format: Uncompressed PCM, MP3, or MP4 (16 bit stereo)
- Audio Sample Rate: 44.1 kHz or 48 kHz
- Container Type: MPEG, MP4, AVI
- Video and audio should be encoded for maximum quality (without going overboard and causing decoding problems, of course). As a guideline, we suggest encoding MPEG-2 video at a bit-rate between 2 Mbps and 5 Mbps, and MP3 audio at 256 kbps or higher. Variable bitrate encoding is fine.
- Do not use joint stereo for your audio encode. This will create noticeable audio artifacts, and your video may be disqualified.
- Should include 2 seconds of black silence before and after your AMV. This does not count toward the time limit.
Media
AMVs will be accepted on CD-R, DVD-R, or DVD+R discs only. AMVs must be burned as a file (a data disc), not as an authored VCD or DVD (a video disc).

All media become the property of JAFAX upon submission and will not be returned. Do not send your only copy!

Procedure
Follow these steps to submit your AMV:
-Download, print, and fill out one Entry Form for each AMV being submitted. The form must be completely and accurately filled out. Incomplete or false information is grounds for disqualification.
-Burn your video to a disc (see above for acceptable types) and label it with your real name, the title of your video, and your email address. Use a permanent marker to label your disc. Do not use adhesive labels of any kind.
-Send the disc and Entry Form to the address below.
-Check your email for confirmation that your entry was received. Allow two weeks for a confirmation.
